He Stood There


He stood there,
not with thunder in His voice
not lighting in His eyes,
but with the silence
that settles storms.

He did not rush in
to fix what broke
but sat beside it
like a friend
who doesn't fear the fragments.

His robe gathered dust
from the roads I wandered,
His hands bore stories

of holdong hearts
without letting go.

He asked me nothing,
but knew everything-
not the version I rehearsed,
but the trembling me
beneath the masks.

And still,
He stayed.
Not to shame me
not to scold the dark,
and whisper:
"I'm not leaving."
