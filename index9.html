Would You Pray for Me? PT.2

If your soul is slumped and silent now,
I'll sit with you where shadows bow.
If you can't walk, I'll kneel and pray—
A whispered faith to light your way.

When words pour out and won't make sense,
I'll be the calm, the strong, the fence.
When silence screams more loud than cries,
I'll stay—no questions, just reply.

If your faith is flickering low,
I'll guard the flame until it grows.
Read with you, hold you, give you space,
And wrap your ache in quiet grace.

You don't need to earn this care,
It's love that meets you broken there.
You want to rest, and you don't know how—
But even that is holy now.

So breathe. Or sigh. Or speak. Or wait.
This pain is seen. It's not too late.
For help. For hope. For gentle hands.
For grace that understands.
